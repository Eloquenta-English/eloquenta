Grammar B2

Contrast & Concession

At B2, you need to show that you can hold two ideas in your mind at once. Contrast words show differences. Concession words show that you acknowledge the other side — even before you disagree with it.

Why this = B2-level English

B1: "I liked the film, but it was too long."
B2: "Although the film was over three hours, I found it genuinely engaging. That said, I can understand why some people walked out."

The difference? B2 speakers anticipate the counter-argument. They show range. Examiners reward this in both speaking and writing.

B2 Contrast Words

although / even though contrast concession — Although it was raining, we went out.
however contrast — The hotel was expensive. However, the location was perfect.
on the other hand balancing view — City life is exciting. On the other hand, it can be exhausting.
whereas / while direct comparison — I prefer tea, whereas my brother loves coffee.
despite / in spite of concession — Despite the rain, we enjoyed the trip.
nevertheless / nonetheless formal concession — The task was difficult. Nevertheless, we completed it on time.
that said / having said that softening — The food was excellent. That said, the service could be faster.
Key Phrases B2

Phrases That Do More Than One Job

although contrast concession

"Although I'm not a fan of horror films, I enjoyed that one." — concession (you went against your usual preference)

"Although she's 70, she runs marathons." — contrast (surprising difference)

however contrast topic shift

"The hotel was lovely. However, the beach was far away." — direct contrast

"However, let's move on to the next point." — moving the discussion forward

despite / in spite of concession emphasising obstacles

"Despite the delay, we arrived on time." — concession

"In spite of feeling nervous, she gave an excellent presentation." — emphasising the obstacle overcome

whereas comparison formal writing

"Northern Europe is cold, whereas the south is warm." — geographical comparison

"Online learning offers flexibility, whereas traditional classes provide more social interaction." — essay structure

Speaking Practice B2

Scene 1: A Debate About Social Media

You're in a discussion group at an English school. The topic: "Social media does more harm than good." Show both sides using concession and contrast language.

• What is your opinion on social media's impact on young people?
I think it's complicated. Although social media helps people stay connected, it can also be addictive. On the one hand, it's a great way to share ideas. On the other hand, it can make people feel inadequate. That said, I don't think we should ban it — we just need to use it more carefully.
• Do you think governments should regulate social media companies?
Even though I believe in free speech, I think some regulation is necessary. Despite the benefits of open platforms, there's too much misinformation. Nevertheless, we have to be careful not to give governments too much control over what people can say.
Useful linking words for this scene:
Although...On the one hand...On the other hand...That said...Even though...Despite...Nevertheless...
Speaking Practice B2

Scene 2: Job Interview — Talking About Weaknesses

The interviewer asks about your weaknesses. This is where concession language shines — acknowledge the weakness, then show how you manage it.

• What would you say is your biggest weakness?
I'd say I sometimes take on too much at once. Although I enjoy helping my colleagues, I've learned to be more selective. That said, I've started using a task management system, and it's made a real difference. Despite this weakness, I think my ability to prioritise has improved a lot.
• Tell me about a time you disagreed with a manager.
Once, I thought a project deadline was unrealistic. Even though I respected my manager's experience, I felt I had to speak up. I suggested an alternative timeline, and despite some initial resistance, they agreed to it. In the end, we delivered on the revised date, whereas the original plan would have been impossible.
Useful linking words for this scene:
Although...That said...Even though...despiteIn the end...whereas
Speaking Practice B2

Scene 3: A Difficult Decision

A friend asks about a tough choice you made. Use concession and contrast to show you considered both sides before deciding.

• Have you ever had to choose between two good options? Tell me about it.
Last year, I had to decide between staying in my current job and accepting a new offer. On the one hand, my current job was comfortable and the team was great. On the other hand, the new role offered more responsibility. Although it was a risk, I decided to take the new position. Despite feeling nervous at first, I think it was the right choice.
Useful linking words for this scene:
On the one hand...On the other hand...Although...Despite...In the end...
